Palmer joined Manchester Cityâ€™s academy at Under-8 level and moved through the youth ranks at the club. He captained Cityâ€™s Under-18s during the 2019/20 season and helped the side win the FA Youth Cup, scoring in the final against Chelsea.

Palmer's men's first-team debut arrived the following season when he started in the Carabao Cup fourth round against Burnley. His first senior goal arrived a year later when he struck against Wycombe Wanderers in the same competition.

In 2022/23, the 21-year-old established himself as a regular in the City squad. He made 25 appearances across all competitions and ended the campaign as a Premier League, Champions League and FA Cup winner.

At the start of the next season, immediately prior to his move to Chelsea, he scored in both the Community Shield and UEFA Super Cup.

Having represented England through the junior age groups and played at the European Under-17 Championships, Palmer was part of the side (along with Levi Colwill and Noni Madueke) that won the European Under-21 Championship in the summer of 2023.

He scored in a 3-0 semi-final win over Israel and was in the side that beat Spain in the final.

In September 2023, he scored a fine goal in England Under-21s win against Luxembourg and then the next month, wore the captain's armband in a 9-1 win over Serbia.

In November of the same season, Palmer was a late call-up to the England senior squad for European Championship qualifiers against Malta and North Macedonia, reward for a fine start to his Chelsea career.

He came on in the second half of the Malta match at Wembley to earn his first cap. England won 2-0.

On his third appearance, against Bosnia in June 2024, he slotted his first senior international goal. Following a season in which he scored nine out of nine penalties for Chelsea, it came from the spot. That was in a warm-up game for Euro 2024, with Palmer named in the squad for the tournament in Germany.
